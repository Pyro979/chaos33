    The Game

    33 Seconds of Chaos is a party game for 3–8 players that's easy to learn and quick to play. You draw Chaos cards that tell you how to give clues, and Word cards that everyone tries to guess. Rounds last about 33 seconds so turns are fast and loud. It's the perfect game for when you want something that teaches in two minutes but keeps everyone laughing.

    Designed for:

    • Game nights
    • Parties
    • Family gatherings
    • People who like yelling in a fun way

    How to Play

    1. Draw a Chaos Card to see how you're allowed to give clues. Maybe you'll be a pirate captain, or maybe you'll have to meow after every word.
    2. Draw a Word Card and start the 33 second timer. This is what everyone else needs to guess.
    3. Give clues using your weird Chaos challenge while everyone else shouts guesses. The chaos is the point—embrace it!
    4. Score points when someone guesses correctly. After 10 rounds, the highest score wins.

    TL;DR: Draw a Chaos card, draw a word, shout ridiculous clues for 33 seconds, score points, repeat.

    Meet the Designer

    The designer behind 33 Seconds of Chaos is a longtime D&D player and content creator who knows what makes games fun. But here's the twist: they're making this game with their kids as co-designers and playtesters. The whole family is in on the chaos.

    The goal? A "throw-it-on-the-table, teach-in-2-minutes" party game that still feels clever and chaotic. Something that works for game nights, parties, and family gatherings—wherever people want to laugh and shout together.
